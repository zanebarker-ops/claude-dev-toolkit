# Research Question Bank

## General Interview Principles
- Ask open-ended questions (avoid yes/no)
- Ask about past behavior, not hypothetical futures
- Follow up with "why?" and "tell me more"
- Listen for workarounds — they reveal unmet needs
- Note emotional language — it signals importance

## Generative Research (Discovery Phase)

### Context & Behavior
- Walk me through a typical day when you [activity].
- What tools/apps do you currently use for [task]?
- When was the last time you [did X]? What happened?
- How did you learn to do [task] the way you do it now?
- What does your workspace/environment look like when you [activity]?

### Frustrations
- What's the most annoying part of [task]?
- Tell me about a time when [process] didn't work well.
- If you could wave a magic wand, what would you change?
- What workarounds have you developed?
- What takes longer than it should?

### Motivations
- What made you start using [product/tool]?
- What would make you switch to something else?
- What does "success" look like for this task?
- What's the most important outcome when you [activity]?
- How do you know when you've done [task] well?

### Mental Models
- How would you explain [concept] to a colleague?
- What categories do you think of when you see [items]?
- Where would you expect to find [feature]?
- What do you think would happen if you [action]?

## Evaluative Research (Testing Phase)

### Usability Testing Tasks
- Frame tasks as goals, not instructions:
  - Good: "You want to update your billing address."
  - Bad: "Click Settings, then Profile, then Edit Address."
- Include both common and edge-case scenarios
- Start easy, then increase complexity
- Include at least one task you expect to fail

### Post-Task Questions
- How easy or difficult was that? (1-7 scale)
- What did you expect to happen when you [clicked/tapped]?
- Was anything confusing?
- How would you describe what you just did to a friend?

### Post-Session Questions
- What was your overall impression?
- What was the best part? The worst part?
- How does this compare to [competitor/current tool]?
- Would you use this? Why or why not?
- Who else do you think would benefit from this?

## Survey Questions

### Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)
"How satisfied are you with [product/feature]?" (1-5 scale)

### Net Promoter Score (NPS)
"How likely are you to recommend [product] to a friend or colleague?" (0-10 scale)

### Customer Effort Score (CES)
"[Product] made it easy for me to [task]." (Strongly disagree to Strongly agree)

### System Usability Scale (SUS) — 10 Questions
1. I think I would like to use this system frequently.
2. I found the system unnecessarily complex.
3. I thought the system was easy to use.
4. I think I would need tech support to use this system.
5. I found the various functions were well integrated.
6. I thought there was too much inconsistency.
7. I imagine most people would learn to use this quickly.
8. I found the system very cumbersome to use.
9. I felt very confident using the system.
10. I needed to learn a lot before I could get going.
(Each rated 1-5: Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)

## Research Screener Questions
- How often do you [activity]? (Daily / Weekly / Monthly / Rarely / Never)
- What is your role/job title?
- How long have you been using [product/tool]?
- On a scale of 1-5, how comfortable are you with technology?
- Have you participated in a research study before?

## Interview Debrief Prompts
After each session, answer:
- What surprised you?
- What confirmed your assumptions?
- What new questions emerged?
- What quotes stood out?
- What should we explore in the next session?
